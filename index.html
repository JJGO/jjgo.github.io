Jose Javier Gonzalez Ortiz

  • PhD Student, MIT
I am a PhD student at MIT CSAIL in the CAML group, advised by John Guttag and Adrian Dalca. I’m interested in efficient machine learning methods, investigating how to make current machine learning more computationally efficient and how to reduce training costs from an algorithmic and model design point of view. Recent projects I’ve worked focus on amortized model learning and in-context learning approaches, where many deep learning models are learned jointly substantially reducing computational costs. Previously, I’ve worked on pruning methods and distributed training for deep neural networks.
At MIT, I have also had the opportunity to teach several classes. I co-organized Missing Semester, a lecture series on programmer tools often left untaught in modern CS curriculums. I was also a TA for MIT’s graduate distributed systems class 6.824 as well as Intro to Deep Learning 6.S191. Before joining MIT, I received a BSc in Telematics Engineering from ICAI in Spain. During my undergrad I interned at CERN Openlab and studied for one year at the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor under an exchange program.
